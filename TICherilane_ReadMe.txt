Cherilane is intended as a mildly adult "romantic-encounters" style NPC for BG1:RE. She is a bard, formerly employed as a poetry and music instructor for <CHARNAME>, who appears in up to six short encounters; Candlekeep Storeroom, Candlekeep Inn, Nashkel Fair, Nashkel Fair's Black Lotus Tent, Davaeorn's Quarters in Cloakwood Mines, and the Third Floor of the Duchal Palace. Cherilane has been in love with <CHARNAME> since he/she came of age, having been one of his/her instructors for years. Cherilane will romance male or female player characters, although some content varies based on the player character's gender.

Sources:
The following poems are quoted in part or whole:

 "Vivamus, mea Lesbia, Atque Amemus" and "When I Left You" by Catullus (Hûneth) 
 "Love's Philosophy" by Percival Bysshe Shelley (Hyalma)
 "Come Slowly, Eden" by Emily Dickinson (Heruion)
 "How Do I Love Thee" by Elizabeth Barrett Browning (Baran)
 "To His Mistress Going to Bed" by John Donne (Tyelde)
 "Lady, i will touch you" by e.e.cummings (Teli)
 An Excerpt from _The Ballad of Reading Gaol_ by Oscar Wilde (Braig)
 "In Memoriam A.H.H." by Alfred, Lord Tennyson (Gelirion)

Each poet has been renamed to avoid breaking the fourth wall. Names are variants of the original English translated into Tolkien's Elvish dialect Quenya. 

Cherilane's soundset ("TICHf") is custom recorded. The portrait is FBard1 (creator?).
